# Auki Glossary

**Source:** Auki Labs technical glossary ([whitepaper reference](https://auki.gitbook.io/whitepaper))
**Exhibits:** Future Foresight (dominant), Shared Prosperity (implicit via openness)
**Integration quality:** Primary-dominant — foundation-laying definitions

## Why this is foundational reference material

The glossary is the most concentrated source of Auki-native vocabulary. It defines how Auki-ers think about spatial computing, the posemesh, economics, and governance — in precise, one-line-or-less definitions.

The lens's vocabulary map is drawn primarily from this document.

## Observable skills from the vanguard domains

**Strong (Future Foresight domain):**
- Systems design — every term is named as a component in a coherent system
- Ethical judgment and governance — definitions of `trustless`, `reputation`, `organization` encode the governance architecture
- Strategic thinking — the distinction between `supply participant` and `demand participant` encodes the market design
- Critical thinking — precision choices (e.g., "pose" vs "position" — one includes rotation, the other doesn't)

**Implicit (Shared Prosperity domain):**
- Transparency and accountability — the glossary itself, public and cross-referenced
- Economic and tokenomic design — `burn`, `credit`, `deflationary mint`, `treasury`, `reputation` all defined
- Inclusivity — open standards vocabulary

**Flash (Narrative Dynamics domain):**
- One memorable compression: *"The posemesh... a mesh of machines reasoning about pose."* — that single phrase shows the rhetorical ceiling of what Auki wants its vocabulary to achieve. Precise + evocative.

## What the lens learns from this exemplar

1. **Use precise terms for precise things.** Not "device" — `participant`. Not "coordinate system" — `domain`. The specificity is the point.
2. **Definitions are short and complete.** "A pose is a position and rotation within a coordinate system." Eleven words. Nothing missing. Nothing extra. This is the compression target for lens output.
3. **Cross-reference density.** Every term links to other terms. Auki thinks in a connected vocabulary network. The lens should likewise: when using one Auki term, the adjacent terms are available for reference.
4. **One flash of compression per section.** The "mesh of machines reasoning about pose" line is rare. Don't force it. When the right compression arrives, use it.

## When to reach for this exemplar

Always. The glossary is the source of truth for Auki-native vocabulary. Any Radiant output that references architecture, economics, governance, or technical concepts should pull from here. If a term is defined here, use Auki's definition. If it's not here, consider whether a generic English word is more appropriate than inventing Auki-adjacent vocabulary.
