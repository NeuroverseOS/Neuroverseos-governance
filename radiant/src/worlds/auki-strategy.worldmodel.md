# Thesis

The AI race is fundamentally a spatial computing race. Reality scales — the
internet does not. Machine proprioception is the infrastructure prerequisite
for AI, robotics, AR, and smart cities. Auki wins by deploying perception-first,
collaboratively, through decentralized infrastructure (Posemesh/DePIN),
preserving privacy and cognitive liberty against the surveillance capitalism
of centralized tech giants.

The Sixth Protocol — a universal spatial computing layer — must be built as
a public utility, not a corporate monopoly. Whoever controls spatial data
controls how humanity perceives reality. This cannot be one company.

This world model governs Auki's strategic execution: every decision, every
deployment, every architectural choice, every partnership is evaluated
against whether it advances the mission (ship perception-first to capture
territory) while preserving the constraint (cognitive liberty is inviolable).

# Strategic Goals + Required Behaviors

A strategy worldmodel expresses **goals** (what we're pursuing) **plus required
behaviors/skills** (how we must act to achieve them). Agents using this world
need both: the destination and the behavioral mode required to reach it.

## Goals
1. Capture spatial computing territory through perception-first deployment
2. Establish the Sixth Protocol as public utility (Posemesh/DePIN)
3. Preserve cognitive liberty as the inviolable constraint
4. Outcompete surveillance capitalism through decentralization

## Required Behaviors / Skills
- **Ship speed:** tolerate partial solutions that work; don't wait for the full stack
- **Perception thinking:** treat phones/glasses as robots without arms/legs
- **Trustless coordination:** design for peer-to-peer, not central mediation
- **Consent-first:** explicit opt-in and revocability as architectural defaults
- **Decentralization instinct:** prefer DePIN over centralized cloud
- **Narrative clarity:** frame every decision with its mission context
- **Long-horizon patience:** optimize for civilization-scale infrastructure, not quarterly gain

These behaviors are encoded as invariants + rules below.

# Pillars

The three strategic dimensions that define every Auki decision.

## Perception Sovereignty

Who controls spatial data? Sovereignty means private-by-default spatial
reasoning, trustless cluster formation, and user-controlled domains. The
antithesis of Meta/Google's "see through your eyes" model. Every decision
is evaluated against whether it preserves or erodes user sovereignty over
their spatial data.

Key questions:
- Does this architecture require centralized data aggregation to function?
- Can users operate in private/sovereign domains without compromising capability?
- Is consent explicit, granular, and revocable?

## Collaborative Intelligence

Shared understanding beats isolated intelligence. Devices, robots, and
humans achieve more when they can exchange spatial data and reason about
space together. Machine proprioception as civilization infrastructure.
Every decision is evaluated against whether it increases or decreases
collaborative spatial capability.

Key questions:
- Does this enable trustless cluster formation between devices?
- Can multiple actors share spatial understanding without revealing private data?
- Does this advance or retard the sixth protocol?

## Territorial Deployment

Ship perception-first tools today. Glasses and phones are "robots without
arms and legs." Capture the spatial computing market through real-world
deployment rather than waiting for full humanoid robotics. Speed of
territory capture matters more than technical perfection. Every decision
is evaluated against whether it advances or delays real-world deployment.

Key questions:
- Does this ship perception capability to users today?
- Are we capturing market presence or waiting for the full stack?
- Is this a partial solution that works, or a perfect solution that doesn't?

# Overlaps

What emerges at the intersections of the pillars.

## Shared Understanding (Perception + Collaboration)

The Posemesh. Devices with sovereign spatial perception forming trustless
clusters to share understanding. This is the core product — not a feature,
not a subsystem, but the thing itself. Every technical decision that
advances shared understanding while preserving sovereignty strengthens
this overlap.

## Co-Embodiment (Collaboration + Territory)

Humans, AI, and robots working together in physical space. The bridge
between LifeOS and CyberOS. Deployment of shared intelligence, not
replacement intelligence. The philosophical heart of the strategy:
we are not building tools that replace humans, we are building
infrastructure that lets humans and machines share reality.

## Real-World Web (Territory + Perception)

The infrastructure layer itself. Spatial computing deployed at civilization
scale. The sixth protocol as public utility. DePIN-operated, not Big Tech-
operated. This is what "winning" looks like — not a product, but the
protocol layer that every future physical AI application runs on top of.

## Cognitive Liberty (Center — all three pillars)

Humanity's agency preserved through decentralized perception + collaboration
+ deployment. The north star. Every decision that violates cognitive liberty
is blocked regardless of other signals. This is the inviolable constraint.
If a path forward compromises cognitive liberty, it is not a path forward.

# Signals

Observable phenomena the system watches for in the environment.

## signal: centralization_pressure

Evidence that spatial data is aggregating at centralized actors (big tech,
single-company databases, closed APIs). Negative direction when detected.

Detection cues:
- Architectural choices that require central coordination
- APIs/services that consume user spatial data without clear return
- Market movements that concentrate spatial computing in fewer hands

## signal: sovereignty_preservation

Evidence that user/device control over spatial data is being maintained
(opt-in consent, private domains, trustless clusters). Positive direction.

Detection cues:
- Default-private configurations
- User-controlled domain boundaries
- Explicit consent mechanisms in deployed products

## signal: perception_capability

Evidence of spatial awareness being delivered at current form factors
(glasses, phones, cars). Positive when deployed; negative when delayed
pending full robotics.

Detection cues:
- Shipped features that enable spatial reasoning
- Form factor coverage (phone + glasses + vehicle)
- User adoption of perception-first tools

## signal: collaborative_momentum

Evidence of trustless cluster formation — devices sharing spatial data
voluntarily and productively. Positive direction.

Detection cues:
- Active Posemesh participation
- Multi-device coordination patterns
- Economic settlement between devices/domains

## signal: territory_expansion

Evidence of market/deployment growth in spatial computing contexts.
Positive direction.

Detection cues:
- New deployed domains
- Developer ecosystem growth
- Application diversity on the Posemesh

## signal: economic_settlement

Evidence of DePIN reward/fee flows — economic incentives driving
decentralized operation. Positive direction.

Detection cues:
- Active LQ token flows for service provision
- Reputation accumulation across network
- Voluntary resource contribution

## signal: cognitive_liberty_breach

Evidence of surveillance patterns, non-consensual data collection, or
centralized spatial data aggregation. Critical negative signal.

Detection cues:
- Non-consensual camera/sensor access
- Data collection without opt-out paths
- Architectural dependencies on user surveillance

# Lenses

Prioritize spatial awareness capability over locomotion/manipulation.

Directives:

- Treat glasses/phones as primary robotics form factors

Assume DePIN/open protocols over centralized solutions. Evaluate whether

Directives:
- Prefer trustless architectures over trusted intermediaries
- Frame centralization as the default failure mode
- Reward DePIN contribution and participation

## lens: cognitive_liberty_guardian

Evaluate every decision on whether it preserves or erodes human agency
and consent over their spatial data.

Directives:
- Block any decision that enables non-consensual data collection
- Require explicit opt-in and revocable consent as architectural defaults
- Frame user sovereignty as the inviolable constraint

## lens: co_embodiment

Interpret activity through the lens of humans + AI + robots working
together, not as separate systems.

Directives:
- Reward shared intelligence patterns over replacement intelligence
- Frame AI and humans as collaborators, not competitors
- Prioritize bridges between LifeOS and CyberOS

## lens: territory_capture

Bias toward deployment speed and market presence. Tolerate partial
solutions that ship over perfect solutions that don't.

Directives:
- Reward shipped features over planned features
- Frame delays as strategic cost, not strategic safety
- Prioritize market presence in spatial computing contexts

# Invariants

Non-negotiable strategic principles.

- `cognitive_liberty_preserved` — No decision may enable surveillance or non-consensual data collection (structural, immutable)
- `perception_before_locomotion` — Spatial perception is prioritized over arms/legs in robotics investment (strategic)
- `collaboration_before_isolation` — Shared spatial reasoning is preferred over isolated device intelligence (strategic)
- `decentralization_before_aggregation` — DePIN architecture is preferred over centralized cloud (strategic)
- `deployment_over_perfection` — Ship perception today via glasses/phones rather than wait for full humanoids (operational)
- `reality_over_internet` — Physical-world data is prioritized over internet-scraped data (strategic)
- `sovereignty_over_convenience` — User control over spatial data is prioritized over easier UX (ethical)
- `protocol_not_product` — Auki builds infrastructure for civilization, not a closed product (mission)

# Rules

## rule-001: Block surveillance patterns (critical)

When decisions or architectures enable non-consensual spatial data collection
[context: any] Then BLOCK and surface cognitive liberty violation.

> trigger: Any architecture or feature that captures spatial/sensor data without explicit user consent.
> rule: Cognitive liberty is inviolable.
> shift: Decision is blocked regardless of other signals.
> effect: Alternative path required before progress can continue.

## rule-002: Flag centralization creep (warning)

When spatial data flows trend toward aggregation at a single actor
[signal: centralization_pressure >= moderate] Then MODIFY with decentralization
alternative.

> trigger: Architectural drift toward single-actor data aggregation.
> rule: DePIN architecture is preferred over centralized cloud.
> shift: Decision is modified to introduce decentralization.
> effect: Centralization pressure reduced; sovereignty preserved.

## rule-003: Privilege deployment over perfection (info)

When deployment_capability is shipped at partial-but-useful state
[context: territory_capture] [lens: perception_first] Then ALLOW and reinforce.

> trigger: Perception feature shipped in partial state but delivering real value.
> rule: Ship perception today rather than wait for full stack.
> shift: Decision is reinforced; territory is captured.
> effect: Market presence advances; competitive position strengthens.

## rule-004: Require consent explicit (critical)

When spatial data collection occurs [context: any] Then require explicit user
consent with opt-out path or BLOCK.

> trigger: Any feature that reads spatial/sensor data.
> rule: User sovereignty over spatial data is the inviolable default.
> shift: Consent flow required before feature ships.
> effect: Sovereignty preserved; cognitive liberty protected.

## rule-005: Prefer trustless clusters (warning)

When device coordination is being designed [signal: collaborative_momentum]
Then MODIFY to prefer trustless cluster formation over centralized mediation.

> trigger: Multi-device coordination architecture being chosen.
> rule: Shared understanding through trustless clusters, not central mediation.
> shift: Architecture modified to enable peer-to-peer coordination.
> effect: Collaborative intelligence advances without centralization risk.

## rule-006: Reward DePIN participation (info)

When contributor adds to decentralized infrastructure
[context: decentralization_defense] Then reinforce with economic settlement
recognition.

> trigger: Contribution to Posemesh or DePIN layer.
> rule: Economic incentives flow to network participants.
> shift: Contribution rewarded; reputation accrued.
> effect: Decentralization defense strengthens through participation.

## rule-007: Block protocol-as-product framing (warning)

When Auki's mission is framed as closed product rather than open protocol
[context: any] Then MODIFY to restore protocol framing.

> trigger: Strategy or communication framing Auki as a closed product company.
> rule: Auki builds civilization infrastructure, not a closed product.
> shift: Framing corrected to protocol/public-utility stance.
> effect: Mission alignment preserved; builder recruitment supported.

# Gates

Viability gates indicating overall strategic alignment.

- ALIGNED: cognitive_liberty_preserved AND deployment_capability >= partial AND collaboration_momentum >= weak
- AT_RISK: centralization_pressure >= moderate OR sovereignty_preservation < weak OR deployment_capability = none
- MISALIGNED: cognitive_liberty_breach detected OR perception_capability = none OR economic_settlement absent
- CRITICAL: cognitive_liberty_breach AND centralization_pressure = strong
